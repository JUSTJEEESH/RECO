    Why island power costs more — and what brings it down

    Fuel arrives by ship

    Roatán isn't connected to the mainland grid. Every kWh from the LPG plant carries import, shipping, and storage costs — that's the fuel adjustment on your bill.

    Sun and wind are free

    Every MWh from the solar park and wind farm displaces imported fuel. Growing renewables is the single biggest lever on your rate.

    You have a lever too

    AC settings, fridge seals, water heaters and pool pumps drive island bills. Our efficiency guide shows the five changes with the biggest payback.

    How Roatán is powered

    An isolated island grid, run in the open: what generates your electricity today and where it's heading.

    Today's generation curve

    Demo data

    Island demand (MW) across the day. Solar output (shaded) peaks at midday, displacing LPG generation.

    Generation fleet

    • Pristine Bay LPG plant — 28 MW

      Four high-efficiency Wärtsilä engines burning cleaner LPG — the island's backbone and its most expensive fuel.

    • Solar park — 7 MW

      Grid-scale solar that carries a large share of daytime demand at near-zero marginal cost.

    • Trade Winds wind farm — 3.9 MW

      Turbines at Brass Hill harvesting the same trade winds that named the island's east end.

    • GEMS battery storage

      Batteries smooth clouds and gusts, keep the grid stable, and shift solar energy into the evening.

    Milestones

    1. 1992
      RECO founded

      Community-managed electric service begins for the island of Roatán.

    2. 2016
      Trade Winds comes online

      First utility-scale wind on the island at Brass Hill.

    3. 2019
      Santa Elena electrified

      US$9M project with 4 km of submarine cable brings reliable power to Santa Elena.

    4. 2021
      Solar + storage era

      7 MW solar park and GEMS batteries integrate into daily dispatch.

    5. 2026
      This proposal

      A digital front door that matches the engineering behind it: transparency, alerts, and self-service for every customer.
