    Who gets power back first — and why

    Restoration order is published policy, not favoritism. We repair in the sequence that restores the most people, fastest.

    1. The plant, then critical servicesGeneration, the hospital, water pumping, clinics, and emergency communications.
    2. Main feedersThe east–west backbone lines that each bring back thousands of customers at once.
    3. Neighborhood linesBranch lines serving communities, largest groups first.
    4. Individual servicesSingle homes with damaged connections — a crew comes to every single one.

    Prepare your home

    Before the storm

    • Charge phones, power banks, and lamps; freeze bottles of water
    • Update your phone number with us to receive alerts
    • Photograph your meter and breaker panel
    • Prepaid customers: top up — reloading may be harder after

    During

    • Stay away from windows; unplug sensitive appliances
    • Never touch downed lines — report them by WhatsApp if safe
    • Run generators OUTSIDE only — carbon monoxide kills silently

    After

    • Treat every line on the ground as live; keep children away
    • Check the outage map before calling — your zone's status is live
    • Food keeps ~4 h in a closed fridge, ~48 h in a full freezer

    The fuel adjustment, opened up

    RECO makes no profit on fuel. It is a pass-through cost: what the ship charges is what you pay, split across every kWh. Here is the actual math, published every month.

    Last 12 months — fuel adjustment (L/kWh)

    August 2025 is a real value from an actual bill: L 1.52/kWh. The rest are illustrative — RECO would publish the real series here.

    LPG delivered (2 shipments)1,420 t
    Landed cost incl. shippingL 21.4 M
    kWh generated from LPG14.9 M
    Renewable kWh (no fuel cost)3.2 M

    Every renewable kWh removes fuel from this equation — that's why the solar park and wind farm lower this line for everyone. Rates are overseen by the CREE, Honduras's energy regulator.

    Why island power costs more — and what brings it down

    Fuel arrives by ship

    Roatán isn't connected to the mainland grid. Every kWh from the LPG plant carries import, shipping, and storage costs — that's the fuel adjustment on your bill.

    Sun and wind are free

    Every MWh from the solar park and wind farm displaces imported fuel. Growing renewables is the single biggest lever on your rate.

    You have a lever too

    AC settings, fridge seals, water heaters and pool pumps drive island bills. Our efficiency guide shows the five changes with the biggest payback.

    How Roatán is powered

    An isolated island grid, run in the open: what generates your electricity today and where it's heading.

    Today's generation curve

    Demo data

    Island demand (MW) across the day. Solar output (shaded) peaks at midday, displacing LPG generation.

    Generation fleet

    • Pristine Bay LPG plant — 28 MW

      Four high-efficiency Wärtsilä engines burning cleaner LPG — the island's backbone and its most expensive fuel.

    • Solar park — 7 MW

      Grid-scale solar that carries a large share of daytime demand at near-zero marginal cost.

    • Trade Winds wind farm — 3.9 MW

      Turbines at Brass Hill harvesting the same trade winds that named the island's east end.

    • GEMS battery storage

      Batteries smooth clouds and gusts, keep the grid stable, and shift solar energy into the evening.
